    Research Experience

    July, 2019 - Aug, 2021

    Designed a state-of-the-art metaheuristic algorithm for the well-known 2L-CVRP problem

    • Description: 2L-CVRP (Iori at al. Transportation Science), a well-known NP-Hard problem composed of the CVRP and the two-dimensional bin packing problem, has been widely studied for 14 years. Hundreds of journal and conference papers have been published, including 2 paper in Transportation Science, 9 papers in European Journal of Operations Research (EJOR), 4 papers in COR, 3 papers in Ann Oper Res, etc.
    • Highlight 1: designed a state-of-the-art metaheuristic called AMA-ENS to solve the 2L-CVRP, which signiﬁcantly outperforms the existing state-of-the-art heuristic algorithms in the literature, including SA (Wei et al. EJOR), VNS (Wei at al. EJOR), BR-LNS(Dominguez at al. EJOR), etc.
    • Highlight 2: found new best-known solutions (BKS) for most instances of the 2L-CVRP bench-mark data set, and achieved an average improvement of nearly 0.7%-1.4% on the current best level. Refer to Technical Report [PDF] for detailed results.
    • Highlight 3: extended the 2L-CVRP model under the consideration of time-dependent travel times, called 2L-TDCVRP, and solved it eﬃciently by AMA-ENS.
    • Achievements: achieved a leading edge in the study of 2L-CVRP, and submitted my work to European Journal of Operations Research and Annals of Operations Research respectively, both of which have received revision comments.

    July, 2019 - Nov, 2021

    Designed a state-of-the-art metaheuristic algorithm for the well-known 3L-CVRP problem

    • Description: 3L-CVRP (Gendreau at el. Transportation Science), a well-known NP-Hard problem consisting of CVRP and three-dimensional bin packing problem, is more complex than 2L-CVRP due to more demanding constraints, like the 3D scene construction, the strict loading requirement of fragility, support, and last-in-ﬁrst-out order. A lot of research work on 3L-CVRP has been published in the Transportation Science, Transportation Research Part B, EJOR, COR etc.
    • Highlight 1: designed a novel dimensionality reduction three-dimensional bin packing algorithm, which works by transforming three-dimensional packing into two-dimensional packing.
    • Highlight 2: proposed a new state-of-the-art metaheuristic able to outperform the existing advanced algorithms for 3L-CVRP, and found new best solutions for the large-scale 3L-CVRP benchmark instances. Refer to Technical Report [PDF] for detailed results.
    • Highlight 3: formulated the 3L-CVRP with time-dependent travel times (3L-TDCVRP) considering practical traﬃc jam, which deﬁnes the speed functions on the complete graph, thus overcoming the inadequacy of the original 3L-CVRP model. Notably, same attempts have been made in a lot of studies recently, like the TDCAEP problem (Vidal at al. 2021. Transportation Science), MT-TDVRPTW problem (Pan at al. 2021. EJOR), etc.
    • Achievements: achieved a leading edge in the study of 3L-CVRP. My new paper, titled Three-dimensional Capacitated Vehicle Routing Problem with Time-Dependent Travel Times, is going to be ﬁnished, and to be submitted to Transportation Research Part B: Methodological.

    July, 2019 - Aug, 2021

    Proposed a metaheuristic algorithm called AMA-ENS able to compete with the state-of-the-art SISR (Christiaens et al. 2020) and FILO (Accorsi at al. 2021) for the well-known CVRP problem

    • Description: CVRP, the cornerstone of VRP-related research problems in Operations Research, has strong application value in the logistics industry. After 60 years of research, now, the diﬃculty lies in solving it in large-scale and super large-scale road networks.
    • Highlight 1: designed a new geometric operator to optimize the searching process.
    • Highlight 2: designed a destruction and reconstruction operator to improve the local search eﬃciency.
    • Achievements: On the newly published CVRP benchmark data set (Uchoa at al. 2017. EJOR), AMA-ENS could compete with the SISR algorithm (Christiaens et al. 2020. Transportation Science) and FILO (Accorsi et al. 2021. Transportation Science), and even gains a slight advantage over them in view of the average gap to BKS. Refer to Technical Report [PDF] for detailed results.
